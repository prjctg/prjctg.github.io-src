# Using the Player

The player is the main experience on Anı⃰mr. An animation plays in sync with the music, and a set of controls lets you manage playback, adjust the audio, switch content, and customise the display.

---

## Basic playback controls

The control bar appears at the bottom of the screen. It contains:

| Control | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Play / Pause button** | Starts or pauses playback |
| **Progress bar** | Shows how far through the song you are. Click anywhere on the bar to jump to that position. |
| **Volume icon** | Click to mute or unmute. The slider next to it adjusts the volume level. |
| **Time display** | Shows the current position and total duration |

---

## Playback speed

Open the **settings menu** (the gear icon in the control bar) and select **Playback speed** to choose how fast the audio and animation play. Options include slower speeds for study or learning, normal speed, and faster speeds. Both the audio and the animation change together.

---

## Switching songs and animations

You can change what is playing without stopping the music.

### Song selector

A dropdown in the control area lets you pick a different song. Switching songs will reload the animation to match the new song.

### Animation selector

A separate dropdown lists all animations available for the current song. Select a different animation and it will swap in smoothly — the audio keeps playing uninterrupted, and the new animation syncs to the current position.

---

## Animation settings

Some animations have parameters you can adjust. When an animation has configurable options, a gear button appears in the control bar area. Click it to open the animation settings panel.

Parameters vary by animation and can include things like:

- Which lyric channel or language to display
- Visual style or colour options
- Numeric sliders for intensity, size, or speed
- Toggles to show or hide parts of the animation

Changes take effect immediately. Look for a **Reset to Defaults** option to restore the animation's original settings.

---

## Video background

Anı⃰mr can show the song's music video alongside or behind the animation. Open the settings menu to control this:

- **Load video/audio** — toggles whether the video is loaded at all
- **Video position** — choose between **Hidden** (audio only), **Default** (video shown in its own area), or **Background** (video behind the animation)
- **Video transparency** — when using Background mode, set how visible the video is (from opaque to mostly transparent)
- **Video fit** — when using Background mode, choose **Fill** (covers the full area), **Fit Width**, or **Fit Height**

If the video fails to load, a **Switch to audio only** button appears automatically.

---

## Fullscreen mode

Click the fullscreen button (at the right end of the control bar) to expand the animation to fill your entire screen. The navigation bar hides, giving the animation the full display. Click the button again or press **Escape** to exit fullscreen.

---

## Skip Intro button

If a song has an intro before the music or lyrics start, a **Skip Intro** button appears on screen. Click it to jump straight to where the content begins. This button disappears once you pass the intro point.

The Skip Intro button is shown by default. You can turn it off in [Preferences](./settings).

---

## Pinning the controls

By default, the control bar fades out after a few seconds of inactivity while playing. To keep it visible at all times, open the settings menu and enable **Pin controls**. You can also set this permanently in [Preferences](./settings).

---

## Mobile

On mobile, the controls work the same way but are optimised for touch:

- **Tap the screen** to show or hide the control bar.
- All buttons and sliders are touch-friendly.

---

## Minimising the player

The **chevron-down** button at the right of the control bar minimises the controls. A slim progress bar remains visible at the bottom of the screen so you can still see where you are in the song. Tap or click the bar to restore the full controls.
